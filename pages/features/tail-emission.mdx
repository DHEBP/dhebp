## Tail Emission

A tail emission ensures the long-term sustainability and decentralization of Monero by incentivizing miner participation and maintaining network security even after the maximum supply has been reached. Monero has an infinite tail emission set to 0.6 XMR per 2-minute block. This translates to less than 1% inflation decreasing over time.

Monero's tail emission serves several important purposes:

1. **Incentivizing Miners**: By providing a continuous reward for mining, even after the maximum supply has been reached, the tail emission ensures that miners have an incentive to secure the network. This helps maintain a robust and secure blockchain by encouraging miners to participate in the consensus process.

2. **Sustaining Network Security**: With the tail emission, Monero aims to prevent a scenario where mining rewards significantly drop or cease altogether. Without a continuous reward, miners might abandon the network, potentially leading to a decline in security. The tail emission helps sustain the network's security by ensuring a consistent level of miner participation.

3. **Fair Distribution**: Monero's tail emission also contributes to a fair distribution of the cryptocurrency. By providing ongoing mining rewards, it allows for a more equitable distribution of coins over time, rather than a situation where early adopters hold a disproportionate amount of the supply. This helps promote wider participation and decentralization within the Monero ecosystem.

4. **Reducing Reliance on Transaction Fees**: The tail emission helps reduce the network's reliance on transaction fees as the primary source of miner compensation. This is particularly important for privacy-focused cryptocurrencies like Monero, where transaction amounts and details are intentionally obfuscated. By providing a consistent block reward, Monero reduces the pressure on high transaction fees and ensures that the network remains accessible to users.
